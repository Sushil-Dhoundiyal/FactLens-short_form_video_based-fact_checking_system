Online Fact-Checking Safety Tips

Use these best practices to verify information and stay safe online.

Verify the Source

Always check if the website or author is reputable and has a history of accurate reporting.

Cross-Check Information

Look for multiple reputable sources reporting the same information before trusting it.

Use Fact-Checking Tools

Utilize tools like Snopes, FactCheck.org, or ClaimBuster to investigate questionable claims.

Be Skeptical of Sensational Content

If a headline seems outrageously sensational, pause and verify it before sharing.

Inspect Social Networks

Review the social media accountâ€™s history and interactions to gauge credibility.

Check Publication Dates

Always verify the publication date to avoid outdated or recycled misinformation.

Read Beyond Headlines

Headlines can be misleading; read the entire article before forming conclusions.

Verify Images

Use reverse image search to check if visuals are authentic or manipulated.

Question Your Biases

Be aware of your own biases and seek diverse viewpoints to prevent confirmation bias.

Summary of Media Literacy

Media literacy is the ability to access, analyze, evaluate, create, and act using all forms of communication.
